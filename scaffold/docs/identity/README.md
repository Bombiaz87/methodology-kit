Definition of **{{PROJECT}}'s identity**: who we are, why we exist, where we want
to get to, who we serve and with what promise.

> ℹ️ **Foundational docs — they can go stale.** Mission/vision/values rarely change,
> but the *model* (who we serve, how we acquire, how we operate) evolves: when it diverges, the
> authoritative source is `../DIRECTION.md` (PDR log), not these files. Example banner to
> place at the top of a foundational file overtaken by events:
> `> ⚠️ Model revised — read in light of ../DIRECTION.md [PDR-NNN].`

## Why it's needed

Identity isn't "marketing fluff". It's the concrete reference point for:

- **Deciding what NOT to do** (scope filters: features, customers, partners, markets).
- **Writing consistent copy** (site, materials, pitch).
- **Onboarding** future collaborators (they know what they're building).
- **Investors and partners** (fast alignment on the vision).

> **In plain language.** When you have to say "no" to a feature or a customer, this is where
> you find the yardstick. Without a written identity, every "no" looks arbitrary.

**Freshness contract.** It has to be kept **alive**: reread it **every 6-12 months** and update it if
the project's reality has drifted. An identity that's never reread becomes a document that lies.

## Methodology

One at a time, in order: **Mission → Vision → Values → Ideal customer → Promise**.

Each one **starts from a conversation** (questions → draft → review → final), **not from a dump**.
Same principle as the ADRs/PDRs: no document handed down from on high without discussion.
